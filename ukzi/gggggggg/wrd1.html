Pi-gi, who participated as an assistant producer in the production of the X-Men live-action movie series, became a fan of Marvel as he explored the original Comics to understand the X-Men franchise properly. Piagy said the original comic has all the answers, suggesting that the movie X-Men's story should focus on the conflict between the mutants trying to save humanity and the mutants trying to dominate like the original. As a result, the X-Men were a huge success, earning $424 million worldwide. Impressed by X-Men's performance, Marvel put the weight on Piaget. Pagi was appointed head of Marvel Studios and led the project. PaiGi revamped the basics of Marvel's new filmmaking project.
